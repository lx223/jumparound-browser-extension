Privacy Policy

Last Updated: February 6, 2026

Privacy Summary: JumpAround is a privacy-first browser extension. We do not collect, store, transmit, or share any of your personal data. All tab information is processed locally in your browser's memory and is never saved to disk or sent anywhere.

1. Introduction

JumpAround ("we", "our", or "the extension") is a browser extension that provides quick tab switching functionality using keyboard shortcuts. This privacy policy explains how we handle your information.

2. Information We Do Not Collect

JumpAround does not collect, store, or transmit any personal information. Specifically:

  • We do not collect browsing history
  • We do not collect personal data
  • We do not track your usage
  • We do not use analytics or tracking services
  • We do not use cookies
  • We do not share data with third parties
  • We do not store any data on external servers

3. What the Extension Does

JumpAround is a keyboard-driven tab switcher that helps you quickly navigate between browser tabs. Here's exactly what it does:

Tab Information Access

When you activate the extension (via keyboard shortcut), it temporarily reads the following information from your open tabs:

  • Tab title
  • Tab URL
  • Tab favicon (the small icon)
  • Window ID (to identify which browser window the tab is in)
  • Active status (whether the tab is currently active)
  • Last access time (tracked in memory only, to sort recently used tabs)

Important: This information is read from your browser's memory each time you open the tab switcher and is immediately discarded when you close it. None of this data is saved to disk, logged, or persisted in any way.

Tab Access Time Tracking

The extension tracks when you access tabs (stored in browser memory only) to sort tabs by recency. This helps you find recently used tabs faster. This timestamp data:

  • Is stored only in your browser's volatile memory (RAM)
  • Is cleared when you restart your browser
  • Is never written to disk or persisted anywhere
  • Is never transmitted outside your browser

Tab Switching and Creation

When you select a tab from the switcher, the extension switches to that tab. If you type a search query when no tabs match, the extension can optionally create a new tab with either:

  • A direct URL (if you type a URL)
  • A Google search (if you type a search query)

Required Browser Permissions

  • "tabs" permission: Required to read tab information (title, URL, favicon, etc.) from all your open tabs and to switch between tabs across all windows
  • "activeTab" permission: Required to inject the tab switcher interface into the currently active tab when you press the keyboard shortcut

4. Data Storage

The extension does NOT use any persistent data storage. It does not use:

  • chrome.storage (browser extension storage API)
  • localStorage or sessionStorage
  • IndexedDB
  • Cookies
  • Any file system storage

The only data kept in memory is the tab access timestamps (to sort tabs by recency), which is stored in volatile RAM and is automatically cleared when you restart your browser.

If future versions add user preferences or settings, this privacy policy will be updated to reflect that change, and any such data would remain entirely on your device.

5. Third-Party Services

JumpAround does not integrate with, communicate with, or transmit data to any third-party services or servers.

6. Updates to the Extension

The extension receives updates through your browser's extension store (Chrome Web Store, Firefox Add-ons, etc.). These updates are handled by the browser vendor and follow their standard update mechanisms.
